Internship Case Study

The Evolution of Moro App

A three-stage journey bridging the gap between design freedom and technical performance.

Figma Canva Lovable Google Analytics

Phase 01: Strategic UX Redesign (Figma)

The goal wasn't just a new look, but a better experience. I performed a heuristic evaluation of the legacy site to identify what worked.

By maintaining the familiar structural layout that existing users relied on, I was able to focus on UX best practices: improving information architecture, optimizing button accessibility, and creating a more intuitive visual hierarchy.

Before
After
The Challenge: The initial handoff revealed that the client needed a way for their marketing team to edit seasonal content without touching a single line of code.

Phase 02: Handoff & Accessibility (Canva)

To prioritize the user experience of the internal team, I rebuilt the site using Canva’s website feature. This empowered non-technical staff to make real-time updates to banners and text.

The Roadblock: As the internship progressed, we realized the limitations: Canva didn't allow for custom SEO meta-tags or the granular Google Analytics tracking required for our recruitment KPIs.

Phase 03: The Code-Based Pivot (Lovable)

Refusing to compromise on performance, I used Lovable to migrate the design into a production-ready environment. This allowed me to export clean React and Tailwind CSS.

By moving to a code-based solution, I successfully integrated Google Tag Manager and optimized the site's SEO, ensuring the beautiful design was also a functional business tool.

Final Takeaway

This project was a lesson in adaptability. As a CS student with a UX minor, I learned that the best tool isn't always the one with the most features, but the one that solves the user's ultimate goal—in this case, balancing ease of use with data-driven performance.
